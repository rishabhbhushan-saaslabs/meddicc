Qualification — Is This Worth Pursuing?
Separate signal from noise fast
Identify Pain Metrics Econ. Buyer
▾
What made you take this call today — what's happening inside the business that made this a priority right now?
Identify PainALL
The answer reveals urgency and the real trigger. "We're growing fast" and "our VP just mandated it" require very different sales motions.
Walk me through what your current outbound motion looks like today — how are your reps reaching prospects?
Identify PainALL
Don't assume. Let them describe it. You'll spot the gaps they don't even realise are gaps.
What percentage of your pipeline comes from outbound today, and where do you want it to be in 12 months?
MetricsALL
Industry benchmark: top SDR teams generate 46–73% of total pipeline outbound. Use this gap as your business case anchor.
What's the single biggest thing getting in the way of your reps having more quality conversations each day?
Identify PainALL
Quality conversations per SDR have dropped 55% since 2014 to just 3.6/day. Most prospects feel this pain acutely — make them say it out loud.
Are your SDRs hitting their meeting quotas? What's the gap between booked meetings and meetings that actually hold?
MetricsLeader / Manager
Industry benchmark: 15 meetings booked/month, 80% show rate = 12 held. If they're below this, you have a clear ROI story to tell.
Who else beyond yourself is involved when your company evaluates and buys a new sales tool?
Econ. BuyerALL
Early mapping of the buying committee prevents late-stage surprises. At minimum you want to know: who holds budget, who can veto.
Are you primarily focused on improving customer service, outbound sales, or both — and what's the priority order?

02
Discovery & Demo — Go Deep on the Pain
Connect their world to your solution
Identify Pain Metrics Dec. Criteria
▾
If you could put a number on it — what does a bad connect rate or a wasted dial cost your business per month?
MetricsALL
It takes an average of 18 dials to connect once (Gartner). If they have 10 reps making 60 calls/day, even a 2% improvement in connect rate is hundreds of extra conversations a month. Do the math with them.
How much time are your reps spending on admin — logging calls, updating the CRM, leaving voicemails — instead of actually selling?
Identify PainALL
Manual tasks are the silent killer of SDR productivity. Get them to estimate the hours — that's your automation ROI hook.
When a rep is underperforming, how do you currently diagnose why — is it their talk track, their call volume, the lead quality, or something else?
Identify PainManager / Leader
Managers who can't answer this clearly don't have visibility. That's your conversation intelligence and coaching angle.
What does your current talk-to-listen ratio look like on calls? Are your reps talking more than they're listening?
MetricsLeader / Manager
Research shows a 40:60 rep-to-prospect talk ratio drives better discovery outcomes. Most managers have never measured this — introducing it makes you look like a strategic partner, not a vendor.
How important is CRM integration to you — does your team need calls to log automatically, or are they manually updating records today?
Dec. CriteriaOps / Leader
Are your reps calling across multiple geographies? Do you need local presence — local caller IDs — to improve pick-up rates?
Dec. CriteriaALL
Local presence can lift connect rates significantly for high-volume outbound teams, especially in insurance, real estate and SaaS.
Are your reps using a single channel — calls only — or are they blending calls, SMS, and email in a coordinated sequence?
Identify PainALL
Companies prioritising multi-channel outreach see 56% higher meeting quota achievement. Single-channel teams are leaving pipeline on the table.
Have you looked at any other solutions for this? What did you evaluate, and what made you keep looking?
CompetitionALL
Early competitive intel lets you position proactively. Their answer tells you the exact criteria they'll judge you on.
03
Metrics — Quantify the Opportunity
No number, no urgency. No urgency, no deal.
Metrics
▾
What KPIs does your leadership team hold you accountable to — connect rate, meetings booked, pipeline generated, or conversion to close?
MetricsALL
Their answer tells you which metric to build your ROI case around. Pipeline generated is the ultimate SDR metric — median is $3M annually per SDR (The Bridge Group).
How many dials does it currently take your team to get one meaningful conversation — and how does that compare to where you want to be?
MetricsLeader / Manager
Industry average: 18 dials per connect (Gartner), 4.4 connects per 100 touches (The Bridge Group). If they're worse than this, the improvement potential is concrete and compelling.
What's your current connect-to-meeting conversion rate? And what would a 10-point improvement in that number mean in revenue terms?
MetricsLeader / Manager
Conversation-to-meeting rates range 20–40% for top teams. Quantifying the delta turns "would be nice" into a business case with a dollar sign.
What's your average sales cycle length from first outbound touch to close — and what's the cost of that being 20% longer than it should be?
MetricsALL
B2B SaaS average is 2.5 months. Enterprise deals are 36% longer than 3 years ago. Cycle compression is a powerful ROI lever.
How important is speed-to-lead? What happens to your conversion rate if you reach a new inbound lead within 5 minutes versus 30 minutes?
MetricsALL
Responding within 5 minutes increases conversion by up to 400%. If they're not measuring this, you've just introduced a metric that makes your solution look essential.
On a typical day, how many of your dials actually connect, and how many hit voicemail or ring out? Where does most of your time go?
MetricsSales Rep
If they're spending 60%+ of call time on non-conversations, a predictive or parallel dialer is an obvious win. Let them do the math themselves.
04
Decision Criteria — What "Right" Looks Like to Them
Understand the scorecard before you're on it
Dec. Criteria
▾
When you imagine the perfect outcome from implementing a new dialer — what does your team's day look like that's different from today?
Dec. CriteriaALL
Future-state visioning. Their answer is your demo script — show exactly what they described.
Which dialer model fits your workflow — Power (one agent, one contact, full control), Dynamic (multiple agents on one list), or Predictive (simultaneous multi-line dialing)?
Dec. CriteriaLeader / Ops
The right dialer depends on their team size, compliance environment, and how much context each rep needs before a call connects.
What does your CRM integration need to do — one-way sync, two-way sync, auto-logging, or launching campaigns directly from within the CRM?
Dec. CriteriaOps / Leader
What compliance requirements do you need to meet — 10DLC, STIR/SHAKEN, CNAM, GDPR, TCPA, or call recording laws in specific states?
Dec. CriteriaLeader / Ops
Non-compliant caller IDs get flagged as spam — directly tanking connect rates. This often eliminates vendors early.
For coaching — do you need live call monitoring (listen, whisper, barge), or is post-call analysis (recordings, AI summaries, call scoring) sufficient?
Dec. CriteriaManager / Rep
Would real-time AI assistance during live calls — surfacing objection responses, battlecards, or next-best-action prompts — be valuable for your reps?
Dec. CriteriaManager / Rep
AI-assisted reps book 30–50% more meetings than those using legacy tools. If they haven't thought about this, you're introducing a new value dimension.
If you had to rank your top 3 non-negotiables for choosing a dialer — what would they be?
Dec. CriteriaALL
This is the most direct way to understand the buying scorecard. Write these down — they're the criteria you need to win on in the proposal.
05
Decision Process — Map the Buying Journey
Know how they buy before you try to sell
Dec. Process
▾
Walk me through exactly how a decision like this gets made at your company — what are the steps from "we like this" to "contract signed"?
Dec. ProcessALL
Map every step. Each step is a potential stall point. The more you know now, the fewer surprises later.
Is there a specific date or business event driving your timeline — a new hire plan, a fiscal year boundary, a board review?
Dec. ProcessALL
External deadlines create urgency you don't have to manufacture. Find them early.
If everything goes well from here — what's the realistic timeframe for you to be up and running on a new solution?
Dec. ProcessALL
Will legal or security reviews be part of the process? Do you need SOC 2, GDPR documentation, or a security questionnaire before sign-off?
Dec. ProcessOps / Leader
Security reviews can add 4–8 weeks to a deal. Surface this early so it doesn't blindside you at the finish line.
What would need to be true — in terms of proof, pricing, or people — for you to move forward within the next 30 days?
Dec. ProcessALL
This is a deal-or-no-deal question. Hesitation here tells you where the real blockers are before you've wasted weeks of pipeline.
06
Economic Buyer — Find Who Holds the Yes
Budget authority and final sign-off
Econ. Buyer
▾
Who in your organisation has the final say on a purchase like this — and are they part of our conversation today?
Econ. BuyerALL
If they say "I do" — verify it. If they hesitate, you're not talking to the economic buyer yet.
Is there a budget already set aside for this, or would you need to build the business case and get approval?
Econ. BuyerLeader / Manager
No budget = you're selling twice. Once to your contact, once to get funds approved. Adjust your timeline expectations accordingly.
What does the economic buyer care most about — reducing cost per acquisition, increasing revenue, headcount efficiency, or compliance?
Econ. BuyerALL
Your business case must speak their language, not your contact's. Tailor the ROI story to the specific metric the economic buyer is measured on.
If the numbers stack up, could you get executive buy-in within the next cycle? What would you need from us to make that internal case?
Econ. BuyerALL
You're asking: "Will you champion this internally?" Their willingness to answer tells you whether you have a true champion or just a curious contact.
07
Competition — Know the Battlefield
Who else are they talking to — including "do nothing"
Competition
▾
Are you running a formal evaluation — or is this more exploratory right now? Who else are you speaking with?
CompetitionALL
Knowing whether this is a structured RFP or informal exploration changes your entire approach. Don't assume.
From the other solutions you've looked at — what impressed you, and what left you cold?
CompetitionALL
Their answer is the exact checklist they'll use to evaluate you. Align your demo to what they loved — and address what disappointed them.
Is staying with your current setup — or doing nothing — still a real option on the table?
CompetitionALL
The status quo is always your biggest competitor. If "do nothing" is still viable, you haven't made the cost of inaction vivid enough yet.
If you could only optimise for one thing in your next solution — what would it be, and why hasn't your current tool delivered on that?
CompetitionLeader / Manager
This is your differentiation briefing. Whatever they say is the feature you must demo compellingly and reference in your proposal.
What would make you confident enough to choose us over everything else you're looking at?
CompetitionALL
08
Champion — Build Your Internal Ally
Who sells for you when you're not in the room?
Champion
▾
How does this problem affect you personally — what does it cost you in your day, your team's morale, or your own performance review?
ChampionALL
Champions fight for solutions that solve their personal problems, not just company problems. Find the "I" inside the "we."
If your reps were making 30% more quality conversations per day with half the manual work — what would that change for you as a manager?
ChampionManager / Rep
Paint a vivid personal win. Not just "team hits quota" — but "you get recognised as the person who fixed the productivity problem."
Who else internally would feel the impact of this problem being solved — and would they be open to hearing about it?
ChampionALL
You want multiple champions at different levels. A single champion is a single point of failure — if they leave or change roles, your deal stalls.
If we nail the demo and the numbers make sense — are you the person who would advocate for this internally, and how confident are you that you could get it across the line?
ChampionALL
Confidence here = real champion. Hedging = you need to do more work, or find a stronger ally in the account.

Pre-Trial & Trial — Set Up for a Win
Define success before the clock starts
Metrics Dec. Criteria
▾
Before we start the trial — what specific improvement would make this a clear win for you? What number would need to move?
MetricsALL
Lock in the success metric before the trial, not after. "We'll know it when we see it" is not a success criterion — it's a recipe for post-trial drift.
Which integrations or workflows are critical to test during the trial — what would break the deal if they didn't work as expected?
Dec. CriteriaALL
How many users will be part of the trial, and do you have the technical resources for a smooth setup — or do you need hands-on support from us?
Dec. ProcessOps / Manager
During the trial — how easy was it for your team to get started? Were there any adoption blockers or frustrations in the first week?
Identify PainManager / Leader
Ask this mid-trial, not just at the end. Early friction that goes unaddressed kills deals that should have closed.
Based on what you've seen so far in the trial — are you seeing the improvements you expected? What's the data telling you?
MetricsALL

Proposal, Negotiation & Close
Remove the final blockers. Lock the date.
Dec. Process Econ. Buyer Competition
▾
"Let me recap: the problem is [X], the cost of inaction is [Y], and success looks like [Z]. Does that still reflect where you are?"
Dec. ProcessALL
Always re-anchor before proposing. Things change between discovery and proposal. A misaligned proposal is a wasted proposal.
Does this proposal address everything you said was critical — the features, the integrations, the compliance requirements? Is there anything missing?
Dec. CriteriaALL
Are there any other stakeholders who need to review or approve this before we can move to signature?
Econ. BuyerALL
Late-stage stakeholder surprises are deal killers. Flush them out now.
Where do we stand relative to the other options you've evaluated — is there anything they're offering that you feel we haven't addressed?
CompetitionALL
Never assume you're winning until the contract is signed. Ask directly — it's better to handle a competitive objection now than to lose silently.
Is there anything holding you back from moving forward right now — pricing, contract terms, internal timing, or something else?
Identify PainALL
Ask this directly. Most prospects won't volunteer their final objection — you have to create space for it.
If we can resolve [specific concern], are you ready to move forward before [specific date]? What's the commitment on your side?
Dec. ProcessALL
Always tie a resolution to a commitment and a date. Open-ended agreements don't close deals.
Do we have full buy-in from the decision maker and economic buyer — or is there anyone who could still slow this down that we should get aligned?
ChampionALL
A champion without access to the economic buyer can't close a deal. Make sure your champion has done their internal selling before you expect a signature.
